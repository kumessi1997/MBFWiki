## Summary

The primary English-language product brochure for akaBot, covering the full automation platform from core RPA through to Agentic Process Automation (APA). Published June 2025. This is the most comprehensive single document on what akaBot is and how it sells itself.

The central pitch frames akaBot as a two-layer solution: **RPA** handles the 80% of rule-based tasks, and **Agentic Automation (APA)** handles the 20% of complex tasks requiring judgment. Together they claim to "cover the full automation scenario."

The document is structured around a pain → solution → product → proof narrative, using market statistics to justify the problem, case studies to prove ROI, and a roadmap to signal long-term commitment.

## Agentic Process Automation (APA) — new in 2025

APA is positioned as the next evolution beyond RPA:
- RPA: rule-based, "do" only, manual bot management
- APA: "think, learn, do, make decisions" — handles complex, context-dependent tasks
- APA architecture: Agent Builder (natural language) → Agent Control Room → integrates with RPA, APIs, databases, desktop/web apps

The CV screening use case is the flagship APA demo: 70% screening time saved, 40% quality improvement, 24/7 automated communication with candidates.

## Service model

Assessment → Implementation → Maintenance & Support → Mass Automation (CoE)

Full-service: FPT handles assessment, migration, implementation, 24/7 operation, training.

## Market problem framing (statistics cited)

- Workers waste 12.6 hrs/week on low-value tasks (86%)

- 86% of RPA projects fail due to complexity; 30% fail due to lack of task context

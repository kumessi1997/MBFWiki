## Definition

**Agreement Lifecycle Management (ALM)**, also known as **Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM)**, is the software-managed process of handling agreements from initiation through execution, storage, and post-completion management. Modern ALM platforms digitize the entire contract lifecycle — eliminating paper-based processes, siloed approval chains, and inconsistent document handling.

ALM is the core category that Kyta Platform (FPT IS) competes in, positioned against international platforms (Camunda, SAP, Microsoft) and domestic SaaS (Base, 1Office, Lark).

## Standard lifecycle stages

1. **Initiation** — request creation, form fill (party data, agreement terms)
2. **Drafting** — template-based document generation (AI-assisted)
3. **Negotiation** — track changes, version control, cross-party redlining
4. **Approval** — internal workflow routing (BPM/no-code)
5. **Identity Verification** — eKYC for signatories
6. **Signing** — electronic signature (e-sign, digital signature with TSA/LTV)
7. **Authentication** — legal certification (e.g., Ministry of Industry and Trade in Vietnam)
8. **Storage** — centralized, searchable, encrypted document repository
9. **Post-completion** — renewal reminders, compliance monitoring, reporting

## Key technology components

- **No-code/low-code workflow engine** — non-IT staff can build and modify approval flows
- **eKYC layer** — identity verification before signing
- **Digital signature stack** — TSA (timestamping) + LTV (long-term validation) for legal durability
- **Document authoring** — template engine with data-driven merge fields
- **Storage & search** — centralized repository with full-text search
- **Integration layer (iPaaS)** — connects ALM to ERP, CRM, corebanking, government systems

## Why ALM is a growth market in Vietnam

- **Paper-based culture** — most Vietnamese enterprises still use manual contract processes; COVID-19 accelerated digital shift
- **Legal framework maturing** — Ministry of Industry and Trade certifies e-signature platforms; legal validity now clear
- **Cross-sector demand** — banking (onboarding), real estate (purchase contracts), government (admin documents), manufacturing (procurement), insurance (policy signing)
- **eKYC mandate** — financial institutions increasingly required to verify identities digitally

## Pain points ALM solves

- Delayed approvals (multi-level sign-off taking days/weeks)
- Compliance and legal risk from unsigned or improperly stored documents
- Fragmented workflows across departments
- High admin costs (printing, courier, physical storage)
- Poor processing standardization

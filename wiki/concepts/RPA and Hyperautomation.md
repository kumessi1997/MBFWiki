## RPA and Hyperautomation

**Robotic Process Automation (RPA)** uses software robots to automate repetitive, rule-based digital tasks — filling forms, copying data between systems, triggering actions based on conditions. No physical robots involved.

**Hyperautomation** extends RPA by combining it with AI, ML, process mining, and low-code tools to automate more complex, judgment-intensive processes end-to-end.

## Key players

- Global: UiPath, Automation Anywhere, Blue Prism, Microsoft Power Automate
- Vietnam/Asia: [[akaBot]] (FPT Corporation) — 3,900+ customers, 21 countries

## Typical business outcomes (per akaBot claims)

- 80% reduction in processing time
- 30–50% staffing cost reduction
- 90%+ accuracy on automated processes

## Relevance to AI

RPA is increasingly integrated with LLMs and AI agents. The "agentic automation" framing in the [[akaBot]] catalog positions RPA as a precursor to broader AI agent architectures — where AI systems take multi-step actions autonomously. The gap between RPA and true AI agents is narrowing.

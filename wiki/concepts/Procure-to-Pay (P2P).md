# Procure-to-Pay (P2P)

**Procure-to-Pay (P2P)** is the end-to-end procurement and payment process that covers everything from identifying a purchasing need through to final payment to a supplier. It is a core process in enterprise spend management and a frequent target for digital transformation.

## Process Steps

1. **Budget Control** — Define and approve procurement budgets by department/project
2. **Procurement Planning** — Plan sourcing needs and timelines
3. **Requesting** — Raise purchase requests internally
4. **Sourcing** — Identify and qualify suppliers (RFI/RFQ)
5. **Negotiation** — Bidding rounds to determine best vendor and pricing
6. **Selection** — Evaluate (technical + financial) and select winning supplier
7. **Contract Management** — Draft, approve, and digitally sign contracts
8. **Ordering & Receiving** — Issue purchase orders; receive and confirm goods/services
9. **Payment** — Process and approve payment to supplier
10. **Reports & Analytics** — Track procurement performance, savings, and compliance

## Pain Points of Manual P2P

- Difficult cost control leading to losses
- Time-consuming manual data entry prone to errors
- Difficult to manage both internal processes and supplier relationships simultaneously
- Lack of focus on core business due to administrative overhead

## Benefits of Digitized P2P (Industry Benchmarks)

Based on McKinsey, BCG, EY reports and FPT IS implementation experience:

- **98%** reduction in paperwork
- **99%** reduction in risks and errors
- **0.2–2%** savings on annual procurement costs
- **10x** increase in work productivity
- **92%** control over planned spending

## Vietnam Context

Vietnam enterprises — particularly in manufacturing, distribution, finance, and banking — have high manual procurement reliance. Vietnam's **Luật Đấu thầu** (Bidding Law) adds compliance requirements specific to the market, especially for government-adjacent enterprises, state-owned corporations, and commercial banks.

Electronic Government Procurement (eGP) standards developed for the public sector are increasingly being applied to large private enterprise procurement in Vietnam.

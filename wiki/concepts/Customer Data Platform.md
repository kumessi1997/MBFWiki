## Definition

A **Customer Data Platform (CDP)** is enterprise software that ingests, unifies, and activates customer data from all touchpoints into a single persistent customer profile. Unlike CRM systems (which manage relationships and pipelines) or data warehouses (which store historical data for analysis), a CDP is designed for real-time activation — feeding segmentation, personalization, and campaign execution systems with up-to-date unified profiles.

CDPs are the data backbone of modern loyalty marketing and AI-driven CRM. They enable personalization at scale by resolving customer identity across channels (web, app, POS, email, call center) into one profile.

## Key capabilities

- **Identity resolution**: stitches together data from anonymous, known, and logged-in states into one ID
- **Unified customer profile**: single view of purchase history, loyalty status, engagement, and preferences
- **Real-time data ingestion**: streaming event data from digital and offline touchpoints
- **Segmentation engine**: rule-based and ML-driven audience building
- **Activation**: pushes segments to downstream channels (email, push, ads, CRM, loyalty engine)
- **Consent management**: handles opt-in/opt-out and data privacy compliance

## In the FPT/Utop context

[[Utop]]'s **UEngage** is their CDP + marketing automation module. Their **UCDP** (Unified Customer Data Platform) is the architectural layer underlying the full FPT CX Suite. This makes CDP a foundational component of the FPT CX Suite, not a standalone product — data unification enables LoyaltyOS personalization and UReach's agentic outreach.

UReach's **LAM (Large Action Model)** depends on a quality CDP layer to generate relevant outreach — CDP data quality directly drives AI CRM effectiveness.
